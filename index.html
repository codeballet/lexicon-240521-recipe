Make your own sourdough

Baking your own sourdough bread is healthy and tasty.

Many people seem to feel that it is very difficult to create and maintain a sourdough culture.

However, making your own sourdough is surprisingly easy. Just follow the below recipe and method.

The ingredients

  • A nice glass jar
  • Wholegrain Rye flour (preferrably organic)
  • Water (preferrably filtered)

How to do it

  1. Find a nice jar that you like.
  2. Put a couple of hanful measures of rye flour in the jar
  3. Pour some water into the jar and stir
  4. Adjust the balance of water and flour until the solution gets a thick, creamy consistency, possible to pour from the jar
  5. Let the solution rest for 24 hours in a dark place in room temperature
  6. For seven days, pour out half of the solution, then add a bit of new flour and water

Helpful notes to consider

Do not fill the jar up to more than half. The sourdough will start to rise and needs some room to expand.

If you can, use organic flour and filtered water. This will facilitate the growing of the wild yeast. However, just ordinary wholegrain rye flower and drinking water will also work.

Check the progress and status of the sourdough by smell. After three to four days, it may start to smell a bit unpleasant. However, after around seven days and upwards, the yeast should have killed off most of the odour producing bacteria, and it should have a deep, interesting, almost beer-like smell.
